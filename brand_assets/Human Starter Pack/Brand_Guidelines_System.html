Daily Drip combines street culture momentum with organic, high-end garments. This interactive resource codifies our core assets, strict contrast laws, custom typography, and the 20 fundamental design metrics that separate Daily Drip storefronts from traditional e-commerce.

01 / Concept

Brand Vision

Daily Drip represents structured randomness — clean-cut garments built on physical news drops. The catalog embodies a high-end editorial feel that remains organic and highly detailed.

01.1

Daily Drop Pulse

Our garments reflect active cultural and news-driven topics. The digital experience must feel immediate, responsive, and editorial, as if scanning a daily paper structured for streetwear.

01.2

Organic Geometry

We reconcile organic palettes (sage, clay, charcoal) with sharp geometric layouts. Soft materials are offset by precise hairline grids and tight technical specifications.

01.3

Zero Friction

The user interface is an extension of our thread. Every drag, hover, slide-cart, and micro-interaction must move with the fluid weight of premium heavyweight cotton.

Logo Placement Rules

  • The Contrast Rule: Strictly hotswap to the white drip logo (`logo-long-white.png`) when placing over dark elements (e.g. background `#0e0e0f` in the Arc'teryx storefront theme).
  • Clear Padding: Maintain a minimum clear zone of 24px on all sides of the horizontal logo container in navigation headers.
  • Fixed Heights: Restrict the navigation header logo height to precisely 24px to prevent vertical shift on page scroll.

Logo Violations

  • No Scaling Stretching: Never manually set a logo's width without maintaining aspect-ratio, causing stretching.
  • Incorrect Background Contrasts: Never overlay the black drip logo on dark surfaces or the white drip logo on bright white/cream canvas cards.
  • Raw Text fallbacks: Avoid raw text fallback elements inside high-fidelity showrooms when the image assets are fully loaded and active.

06 / Layout Mathematics

Design Principles & Grids

The Daily Drip design system relies on strict structural constraints rather than decorative decoration. We operate by 4 critical standards based on the E-Commerce Playbook.

01

The 3:4 Portrait Scale

All showroom product images are captured and displayed at a strict 3:4 portrait ratio (e.g. width 600px, height 800px). This maintains massive vertical editorial elegance and matches print model catalogs.

02

45% Whitespace Standard

Layout breathing room must make up at least 45% of the total viewport height in desktop views. Keep margins and section gaps massive (e.g. `margin-bottom: 120px;`) to convey extreme luxury branding.

03

Hairline Border Borders

Never use heavy black borders or strong solid rules. Separate sections and elements using 1px hairline rules structured in low opacity ink charcoal: `hsla(150, 8%, 10%, 0.08)`. This creates invisible structures.

04

Dynamic Cross-Fade Reveals

Product cards must utilize dual-image reveals. The model image sits in front, but hovering seamlessly cross-fades into the flat graphic image of the product to provide quick context on silhouette dimensions.
